# QuantumChaos

The study of quantum chaos seeks to understand how signatures of classical chaotic dynamics emerge in quantum systems, where the notion of trajectories is fundamentally absent. Periodically driven systems provide a particularly rich setting for this investigation, as they naturally lead to discrete-time dynamics governed by Floquet operators. Among such systems, the kicked top has emerged as a paradigmatic model due to its simplicity and its ability to exhibit a transition from regular to chaotic behavior.

The kicked coupled top extends this framework to a bipartite setting, introducing interactions between two angular momenta. This extension allows for the exploration of additional phenomena such as entanglement generation, information scrambling, and the role of conserved quantities in constraining dynamics. Importantly, while the underlying angular momentum algebra admits a full SU(2) symmetry, the presence of anisotropic periodic driving breaks this symmetry down to a residual U(1) subgroup. This symmetry reduction plays a central role in determining the structure of the Hilbert space and the dynamical evolution of the system.

A key objective in the study of quantum chaos is to characterize spectral properties of the system and relate them to universal predictions from random matrix theory. In particular, the level spacing distribution of the Floquet eigenphases provides a powerful diagnostic for distinguishing between integrable and chaotic regimes. The presence or absence of discrete symmetries further determines the appropriate universality class, such as the Circular Orthogonal Ensemble or Circular Unitary Ensemble.

In this report, we present a detailed analysis of the kicked top and kicked coupled top models, focusing on the interplay between symmetry, spectral statistics, and dynamical measures of chaos. By combining analytical insights with numerical results, we aim to provide a coherent understanding of how quantum chaotic behavior emerges in finite-dimensional spin systems and how it is shaped by underlying symmetries.
